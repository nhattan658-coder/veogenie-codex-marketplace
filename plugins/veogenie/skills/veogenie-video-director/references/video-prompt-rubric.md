# Video Prompt Rubric

## Short Ad Structure

For 4-8 second product or social clips:

1. Opening beat: product is visible immediately.
2. Motion beat: one clear camera or product movement.
3. Finish beat: product ends in a clean hero composition.

Avoid long story arcs, multiple locations, or too many actions.

## Prompt Template

```text
Create a [duration] second [aspect ratio] product video.
Subject: [product/person/object], keep [identity details] consistent.
Opening frame: [start state].
Action: [one main movement or transformation].
Camera: [push-in, orbit, dolly, handheld, macro glide, locked-off].
Lighting and scene: [specific environment and mood].
Ending frame: [final hero state].
Constraints: no extra text, no logo changes, no warped shape, no sudden cutaway.
```

## Camera Moves

- Slow push-in: best for premium product reveal.
- Subtle orbit: best for showing shape and material.
- Macro glide: best for texture, ingredients, jewelry, cosmetics, liquid, or packaging detail.
- Locked-off with product motion: best when product identity must remain exact.
- Handheld lifestyle: use only when realism matters more than packaging precision.

## Start And End Frames

- Use a start frame when the opening composition must match a generated image.
- Use an end frame when the final packshot or transformation result must be controlled.
- Do not use both frames unless the user needs a specific transition between two known states.

## Quality Checks

- Product remains visible for most of the clip.
- Motion supports the campaign goal and does not distract from the subject.
- Camera does not crop away important labels or features.
- Lighting stays coherent across the clip.
- The result can be understood with no explanatory text.
